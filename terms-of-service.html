9. Shared apiaries

HiveKeeper allows you to share access to one of your apiaries with other HiveKeeper users, so that (for example) a partner, association secretary, or helper can view or contribute to the records.

The owner keeps ownership

When you invite another beekeeper to your apiary:

  • You remain the owner of all data in that apiary — the hives, the inspection records, the photos, everything
  • The person you invite has access permissions only, not ownership
  • You can revoke their access at any time, and when you do, their access ends immediately with no right to retain or re-use the data

If you're the invitee

Likewise, if another beekeeper grants you access to their apiary:

  • The data in that apiary belongs to them, not you
  • You may view and (if permitted) edit it within HiveKeeper, but you must not export, scrape, screenshot, or otherwise retain a copy of their data for use outside HiveKeeper without their explicit permission
  • If access is revoked, you must stop using any information obtained through that shared access

This clause exists to protect both primary beekeepers (so their records stay theirs) and shared-access users (so expectations are clear).

12. Asian hornet reporting

HiveKeeper includes a feature that lets you report Asian hornet (Vespa velutina) sightings. Because Asian hornet is an invasive species posing serious risk to UK beekeeping, these reports are forwarded on your behalf to the UK Centre for Ecology & Hydrology (CEH) at alertnonnative@ceh.ac.uk, as part of the national invasive species monitoring programme.

When you submit a hornet sighting, the information shared with CEH includes:

  • The date and location of your sighting
  • Any photographs you attached
  • Any notes or description you added
  • Your name and email address, so CEH can follow up if needed

By submitting a hornet sighting through HiveKeeper you consent to this data being shared with CEH for the purpose of invasive species monitoring and response. This data sharing is additional to our general Privacy Policy.

If you'd rather report hornets directly without involving HiveKeeper, you can do so via the Asian Hornet Watch app or the CEH contact above.
